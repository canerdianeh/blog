My wife, the seminary student, put this together for her United Methodist Polity class at SPST. I had to share.

Congratulations!

Today is your day.

You're off to Great Places!

You're off to your new appointment.

You have brains in your head.

You have a Bible in your hand.

You can bring people to Jesus Christ.

You're on your own.  And you know what you know.  And the Bishop is the guy who'll decide where you go.

You'll look up and down churches.  Look'em over with care.  But some you will say, “Do I have to go there?”

With your head full of brains and your Bible in your hand, you're called to go to even  not-so-good churches.

And you may not find you're called to churches where they are all really down.  In that case, of course, you'll head straight out of town.  It's opener there in the wide open air.

Out there things can happen and frequently do to people as brainy and fluent in the Bible as you.

Oh! The Places You'll Go!

You'll be on your way to your new appointment!

You'll be seeing great people!

You'll join the high fliers of great pastors and churches.

You won't lag behind, because you have the right training.

You'll pass the whole gang and God will be reigning

Except when you don't,

Because, sometimes you won't.
I'm sorry to say so but, sadly it's true that Bang ups and Hang-ups can happen to you.

You can get all hung up on your preaching perch.  And your gang will not like you.   And you'll be left in a Lurch

You will come down from the Lurch with an unpleasant bump.  And the chances are, then, that you'll be in a slump.

And when you're in a Slump, you're not in for much fun. Un-slumping yourself is not easily done.

You will come to churches that are sparse.

Some windows are lighted. But mostly are dark.

A place you can sprain both your brain and your ego!  Do you dare to speak out?  Do you dare to preach?  How many members can you lose?  How many can you win?

And if you go in, should you be liberal or conservative?...or in the middle? Or go around back and sneak in with the choir?  Simple it is not, I'm afraid you will find, for a mind-maker-upper to make up their mind when you are a preacher.

Remember the questions they’ll ask at ordination, so that you can avoid embarrassment and frustration.

Do you have faith in Christ? Are you going on to perfection? Do you know the general rules? Did you get them right on the test?

Have you studied our disciplines and polity? Do you approve our church government and polity?

Will you support and maintain them? Will you visit from house to house? Will you observe the following directions?

Be diligent, never be unemployed, never be triflingly employed. Never trifle away time, neither spend any more time at one place than is strictly necessary.

Be punctual. Do everything exactly at the time. And do not mend our rules, but keep them; not for wrath, but for conscience’s sake.

Oh, the places you’ll go! There are sermons to preach! There are souls to be saved! Communion to bless, babies to baptize, couples to marry and funerals to preach. Goals to meet. Bottom line, more seats in the seats!

Except when they’re aren’t. Because sometimes, they’re not.

You’ll get mixed up, of course, as you already know. You’ll get mixed up in many strange charges as you go. So be sure when you step, step with care and great tact and remember that the local congregation is a great balancing act. Just never forget to be dexterous and deft. And never mix up your trustees and your SPRC.

And will you succeed?

Yes! You will, indeed!

Perfection (and appointments) guaranteed.

Kid, you'll move mountains!  So, be your name Hamilton, or Mutti, or Tuell, or Gilmore, VonZardt, or Soeroker-Nicholson, or Winkelpleck, or Lobo, you're off to Great Places!

Today is your day!

Your church is waiting.

So....get your household packed up, you are on your way.
