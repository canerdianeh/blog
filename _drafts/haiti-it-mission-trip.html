Once again this summer, I'll be part of a Church of the Resurrection mission team going down to Petit-Goâve, Haiti, to work on the technology infrastructure for the Methodist Church of Haiti, to enable the church to better communicate and to provide Internet access and computers to the students of the Brakeman School. Resurrection's goal in Haiti is to bring relief to the people of Haiti in the aftermath of the earthquake in January 2010, and to provide the children of Haiti with better education and a means of economic self-sustainability.

One of the challenges I'll be dealing with down there is related to electrical power. Our gear is behind a UPS, but when shore power dips below 96V or so, the UPS goes on battery, even if most of our gear is quite happy running down to about 70VAC input. One problem we discovered at this location was that the power lead-in from the meter to the house is over 200 metres of 6-gauge wire, which alone accounts for 20-25 volts of power being lost to heat in the line. Solving this problem will provide better power at the house, as well as reduce the utility cost being paid by the church.

Success of this mission is of great import, as this particular project in Petit-Goâve is a template for other Methodist Church/school locations throughout Haiti. What we achieve here will likely be replicated throughout the country and have tremendous impact on the lives of thousands of Haitian children.
