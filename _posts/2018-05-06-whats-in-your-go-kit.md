As I prepare for another trip to a customer site, I figured I'd post the contents of my wireless engineering go-kit for the benefit of others wanting to put one together. I've posted previously about my [streaming go-kit](http://blog.ianbeyer.com/2015/01/22/streaming-on-the-go/), which has largely been retired as I'm not doing nearly as much streaming as before, having shifted over to Wi-Fi. Amazon links in this post are affiliate links, and it's where I bought most of this stuff over the course the the last several years. Some of it was freebies from conferences like the [Wireless LAN Professionals Conference](https://www.wlanpros.com/thewlpc/).

What's in the kit?

It will depend largely on the job I'm going to do, but I've got several sub-kits that go in it based on the needs of the job:

## Frame Analysis Sub-Kit:

(this kit has largely been deprecated by my Macbook and Airtool)

- 3 [Netgear A6210](https://amzn.to/2JUHm8b) 2SS 802.11ac adapters for use with Omnipeek - I don't know if the [3SS version A7000](https://amzn.to/2rxHjaR) has requisite drivers for Omnipeek. Word on the street is that Metageek EyePA recently added support for these adapters. AirMagnet can also use these for surveys.
- 1 AirPCAP Adapter for use with Omnipeek (pretty much obsolete at this point)

## Site Survey Sub-Kit:

- (I hope to be able to replace this kit with a [Sidekick](https://www.ekahau.com/products/sidekick/overview/) sooner than later!)
- 3 Ekahau/Proxim NIC-300 adapters
- [Anker USB 3.0 Hub](https://amzn.to/2wip3b1)
- [HubHolster](http://hubholster.com) clips
- [WLAN Pros Site Survey Tray/Walking Desk](https://www.wlanpros.com/shop/custom-survey-tray/) (this has to go in checked luggage due to size - which is where an airline with a generous baggage policy comes in handy, or frequent flier status that gets you checked bags)

## Spectrum Analysis Sub-Kit:

- [Metageek Wi-Spy 2.4x](https://www.metageek.com/products/wi-spy/)
- [Metageek Wi-Spy DBx](https://www.metageek.com/products/wi-spy/)
- [Great Scott Gadgets HackRF One](https://amzn.to/2roYA6R)

## Pentest Sub-Kit:

- Hak5 [Pineapple Tetra](https://www.wifipineapple.com/pages/tetra) Tactical Kit

## Ethernet/Console Sub-Kit:

- 3 [AirConsole Mini 2.0 wireless console adapters](https://www.get-console.com/shop/en/25-airconsole-mini-20)
- 2 [Apple USB Ethernet](https://amzn.to/2HXPd4l) Adapters
- 1 [Apple Thunderbolt Gigabit Ethernet](https://amzn.to/2roNUEp) Adapter

## Test Tools:

- [Netool.io NE1 network tester](https://amzn.to/2JYn2CG)
- [Fluke/Netscout LinkSprinter 300 network tester](https://amzn.to/2KGDyIT)
- [Optical light source/power meter](https://amzn.to/2HTAStE)
- [Fluke Pro3000 Tone/Probe Set](https://amzn.to/2ruwshK) (I've forgotten to bring this WAY too many times, it's a lifesaver)

## Measurement/Installation Tools:

- [Bosch GLM100C laser measurement](https://amzn.to/2rmwrNQ)
- [Bosch GLL55 Laser level](https://amzn.to/2jx3OsK)
- [iFixit 64-bit driver kit](https://amzn.to/2robBgQ)
- [Panduit EGJT termination tool](https://amzn.to/2JX79fL) (Doesn't hurt to carry a handful of [spare jacks](https://amzn.to/2IrJ83j) too) - this takes up way less space/weight than a bag of 8P8C plugs and a crimper, and results in a much better connection.
- [Klein electrician's scissors](https://amzn.to/2HVHPWX)
- [Klein 11-in-1 screw/nut driver](https://amzn.to/2KOWR2N)

- [Brother P-touch Edge label printer](https://amzn.to/2rogPsR) (although thinking of replacing this with a [PT-700](https://amzn.to/2Lf8Qqx)).

## Miscellaneous:

- [Power Tap/Surge Protector](https://amzn.to/2HZ1bdV)
- [USB Charger](https://amzn.to/2rpvSBY) (I carry the [Apple 29W charger](https://amzn.to/2K5QBlU) because my iPhone X and iPad Pro support USB-C PD and I don't have one of the new MacBooks that uses USB-C - I can go from dead to a near full charge on my phone in about an hour, and the iPad charges wicked fast as well. I can also use this to power the [USB3](https://amzn.to/2ruHfIQ), [HDMI](https://amzn.to/2rvUx8F), and USB Ethernet dongles off my iDevices, all at once)
- USB Cables (I love me some Anker cables)
- SD cards (In [Pelican 0915 SD card case](https://amzn.to/2KGaReY))
- Ethernet patch cables
- [10' Slim HDMI cable](https://amzn.to/2HZ66eX) (for presentations or watching Netflix in the hotel room!)
- [Outlet/GFI tester](https://amzn.to/2I1gt1E)
- [Sharpies](https://amzn.to/2JXanjn)
- [AA Batteries](https://amzn.to/2IjkTo0) (your tone/probe set will need 9V, but not often)

## Computing:

- [Microsoft Surface Book](https://amzn.to/2rnWl3p)
- [Apple MacBook Pro 15"](https://amzn.to/2jy2J3T) (upgraded to 802.11ac)
- [Apple iPad Pro 10.5"](https://amzn.to/2FNGHmD)

## Software:

- [Ekahau Site Survey Pro](https://www.ekahau.com/products/ekahau-site-survey/overview/) (Mac/Windows)
- [Savvius Omnipeek](https://www.savvius.com/product/omnipeek/) (Windows)
- [Metageek EyePA](https://www.metageek.com/products/eye-pa/) (Windows)

- [Metageek InSSIDer Office](https://www.metageek.com/products/inssider/) (Windows)

- Microsoft Office (Mac/Windows)
- Microsoft OneDrive (Mac/Windows) (good for sharing frame captures and design files between systems)

## PPE/Safety

- [Fall Protection harness](https://amzn.to/2rVKBWh) (required on boom lifts) with [lanyard](https://amzn.to/2kbCNeG)
- Hard Hat (If I'm flying, I try to obtain one at my destination, either at the jobsite or Home Depot
- [Z87.1 Safety Glasses](https://amzn.to/2Lf8pMV) (polarized shaded glasses really help on a rooftop in blazing sun)
- OSHA 10-hour Construction Safety Certification
- [OSHA Aerial Lift Training & Certification](https://www.certifymeonline.net/training-certification/)
- First Aid/Trauma kit

Depending on the combination of stuff, most of it goes in a [Pelican 1510 carry-on case](https://amzn.to/2JYEWoO) (yes, it all fits - other than the PPE - with some room to spare, especially if you add the [lid organizer](https://amzn.to/2KKN0L8), which is great for keeping small things contained!) . Because some of the devices in there contain lithium batteries, I can't check it - but in that case the scissors and the knife need to go in checked luggage - But if you do some mental calculations and add up what all this stuff costs, you'll see that even without the computers and software, that's not generally something I am willing to let out of my immediate control. I don't bother with TSA locks, because those don't provide any security.

If I only need some of the items, I put them in a smaller nylon case that used to be a carrying case for a projector, which does fit in a checked suitcase. The fiber kit has a dedicated [Pelican 1490 case](https://amzn.to/2wrotru) when not traveling in the 1510.
