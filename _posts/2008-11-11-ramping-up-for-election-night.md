While I'm on the topic of [Slashdotting](http://nerdian.ca/2008/11/05/what-happens-during-a-slashdotting/ "What happens during a slashdotting?"), I had a conversation with [Clif](http://clifguy.com) the other day about what it takes to scale up sites to handle the onslaught of traffic generated by people looking for web coverage of Election Night. It's one of those nasty scalability problems where if you get it wrong, you're utterly screwed and don't get a do-over or a few hours to fix it. If you're in the business of selling eyeballs and your site goes dark during the Big Game, you're pretty much hosed. And broke.

[Data Center Knowledge](http://www.datacenterknowledge.com) has a neat article about [what goes on behind the scenes](http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2008/11/04/political-sites-scale-up-for-election-night-traffic/) to ramp up for an event of this magnitude.
